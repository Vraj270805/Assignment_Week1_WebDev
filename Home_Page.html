Game Overview

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game that originated in England in the 16th century and has since become a popular sport worldwide, particularly in countries like India, Australia, England, Pakistan, South Africa, and the West Indies. The game is played between two teams of eleven players each on a circular or oval-shaped field. The central playing area is a 22-yard pitch with wickets at each end, consisting of three stumps and two bails.

Rules/Mechanics

Objective: The main objective is to score more runs than the opposing team.
Innings: Each team has one or two innings, depending on the match format.
Scoring Runs: Runs are scored by hitting the ball and running between the wickets or by hitting the ball to the boundary.
Wickets: The batting team is out when ten players are dismissed.
